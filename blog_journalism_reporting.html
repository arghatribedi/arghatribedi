Good Journalism Bad Reporting

Bad reporting wastes good journalism. On-ground correspondents often blabber information in response to studio's question. As a result, they run out of time and details don't stick with the audience.

We reproduce this mistake in our workplaces. Every time questioned, we ramble through charts and numbers. We sway away from the actual question leaving it unanswered. How do we tackle this?

We can gain inspiration from Pyramid Principle. Three key features of this principle are: One, state the answer first. Do not reason it yet. Two, mention the number of arguments supporting the answer and state the argument summaries. Three, expand each argument with facts. We even can offer the questioner to choose the argument he/she would like know first.

Structuring engages the listener from the start, boosts retention and makes speaker sound smart. Try it in your next call.
