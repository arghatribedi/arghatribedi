Minimalism changed my life

Daily hunt for toothpaste in college hostel has set me up for success. Minimalism is the art of achieving more in less. It was popularised by a 2016 Netflix documentary. Millionaire youtubers are preaching this lifestyle. But our college life exemplified minimalism better than the internet. Every college-goer is a situational minimalist. Students complete assessments with limited study material, organise parties by crowdsourcing supplies and attend interviews with only three neck-ties per hostel wing. Applying college lifestyle in my later life has yielded following advantages:

  • Decluttered living environment - Do you really need 11 pairs of footwear in your closet? How many do you actively use? Retain few which spark joy(Konmari method) and donate rest.
  • Reduced carbon footprint - It can take upto 2,700 litres water to produce the cotton needed to make a single t-shirt. Curbing the urge to buy every pretty dress on Amazon conserves the real amazon(rainforest).
  • Mitigated paradox of choice - Each morning we spend valuable time choosing an outfit from fifty options. Limiting the wardrobe and picking the outfits for the week on Sunday saves time.
  • Provided flexibility in movement - As a young professional, bogging yourself down with excessive material possessions such as appliances, furniture and a car resist your movement across locations, hence limiting career opportunities. Instead, leverage the shared economy.

In short, minimalism unmasks the why behind your desires. Procure items which you actually require, not which society influences you to. Time and cash are limited. Spend them on experiences, not products.
